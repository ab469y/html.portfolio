Family Diet Plan

Family Members

Profile: Child

Age: 8 years

Activity Level: Moderate

Food Preferences: Balanced diet with a focus on fruits and vegetables.

Profile: Gym Enthusiast

Age: 30 years

Activity Level: High due to regular workouts

Food Preferences: High protein, balanced diet with lean meats, and carbohydrates for energy.

Profile: Elderly Person with Diabetes

Age: 70 years

Activity Level: Low due to age and health condition

Health Condition: Diabetes

Food Preferences: Low-carb, high-fiber, and sugar-free diet to manage diabetes.

Diet Plan

Child

Breakfast: Whole-grain cereal with milk and a serving of fruit.

Lunch: Grilled chicken or fish with brown rice and steamed vegetables.

Snack: Greek yogurt with honey and berries.

Dinner: Pasta with tomato sauce and a side salad.

Gym Enthusiast

Breakfast: Scrambled eggs with spinach and whole-grain toast.

Lunch: Grilled chicken breast with quinoa and broccoli.

Pre-workout Snack: Banana and a handful of almonds.

Post-workout Snack: Protein shake with berries.

Dinner: Baked salmon with sweet potatoes and asparagus.

Elderly Person with Diabetes

Breakfast: Oatmeal with unsweetened almond milk and berries.

Lunch: Lentil soup with a small serving of brown rice.

Snack: Greek yogurt with cucumber and walnuts.

Dinner: Grilled chicken breast with steamed green beans and a side salad.

Food Combinations to Avoid

Avoid combining high-sugar foods with high-fat foods for the elderly person with diabetes. Ensure a balanced and controlled carbohydrate intake.

For all family members, limit processed foods, excessive sugar, and excessive salt.
